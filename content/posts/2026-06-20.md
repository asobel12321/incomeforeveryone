AI-driven restructuring is no longer a theoretical future-work debate; it is showing up in layoff notices, corporate memos, and fresh labor-market research. This week’s updates point to a job market where companies are using automation to justify flatter org charts, smaller teams, and bigger bets on software and AI infrastructure.

---

### Key Stories

- **Robinhood trims 10% of its workforce in a flattening push**
  Robinhood said it will cut about 290 full-time roles, framing the move as an effort to operate more efficiently by flattening management layers. For labor markets, that is another sign that AI-era restructuring is increasingly being sold as “efficiency,” even when the immediate effect is fewer jobs and less room for entry-level advancement.  
  [Trading platform Robinhood cuts 10% of workforce to flatten management layers](https://www.investing.com/news/stock-market-news/robinhood-to-cut-10-of-its-fulltime-workforce-4744185)

- **Intuit cuts about 3,000 jobs while sharpening its AI focus**
  Reuters reported that Intuit is laying off roughly 17% of its global workforce, citing an internal memo that linked the reshaping to streamlining operations and doubling down on AI. This matters for UBI because it captures the harder edge of automation: even profitable companies are using AI investment to rationalize workforce reductions, increasing the case for income supports that are not tied to traditional employment.  
  [Exclusive-Intuit to cut 17% of global jobs to streamline operations, memo shows](https://www.investing.com/news/stock-market-news/exclusiveintuit-to-cut-17-of-global-jobs-to-streamline-operations-memo-shows-4701442)

- **Anthropic backs a $200 million push to study AI’s labor impact**
  Anthropic announced an initial $200 million investment to research AI’s economic effects, alongside a policy argument that people displaced by the technology need economic support. That is one of the clearest signs yet that major AI firms now see labor disruption as a policy problem, not just a byproduct of innovation.  
  [Anthropic pledges $200 million to research AI's economic impact as CEO suggests job loss solutions](https://apnews.com/article/afeb5279eef406980dffa46ff91495e0)

---

### What This Tells Us

The pattern is becoming harder to ignore: companies are cutting staff while accelerating AI adoption, and even AI leaders are openly discussing the need for economic cushioning. For UBI advocates, the message is straightforward — if automation keeps concentrating gains while shrinking payrolls, income support and labor-transition policy will need to scale faster than the layoffs do.
