AI is no longer just reshaping work; it is increasingly shaping who gets laid off, which jobs survive, and how companies justify restructuring. Today’s developments show a labor market where automation is both a productivity strategy and a blunt force behind workforce cuts — exactly the kind of pressure that makes UBI harder to dismiss.

---

### Key Stories

- **Meta AI layoff lawsuit raises the stakes for algorithmic workforce cuts**
  A Reuters analysis published today says Meta employees are struggling to prove that AI systems were used to select workers for layoffs, underscoring how opaque automated decision-making can make it harder for workers to challenge job cuts. For UBI advocates, the case is a warning that as firms automate personnel decisions, displacement may arrive faster than existing safety nets can respond.  
  [Analysis-Meta employees’ lawsuit shows that if AI fires you, proving it is the hard part](https://m.investing.com/news/stock-market-news/analysismeta-employees-lawsuit-shows-that-if-ai-fires-you-proving-it-is-the-hard-part-4805102?ampMode=1)

- **Microsoft’s 4,800-job cut is part of an AI-investment reallocation**
  Reuters reported on July 6 that Microsoft cut about 4,800 jobs as it continued to pour resources into AI infrastructure and reorganize parts of its commercial and Xbox businesses. The message for workers is clear: even profitable firms are using AI-era restructuring to shrink payrolls, not just expand output.  
  [Microsoft to cut 4,800 jobs, overhaul Xbox unit](https://www.investing.com/news/stock-market-news/microsoft-joins-aidriven-tech-layoff-wave-with-4800-job-cuts-4776805)

- **Thomson Reuters trims engineering roles while promising AI-native hiring**
  Reuters reported on July 13 that Thomson Reuters is cutting a small number of engineering jobs while saying it expects to add more than 250 net-new engineering roles over two years, mostly senior and “AI-native.” That pattern points to a broader workforce reset: fewer traditional roles, more specialized AI-centered labor, and more risk for workers whose skills are easier to automate or repackage.  
  [Thomson Reuters to cut ’small number’ of engineering jobs](https://www.investing.com/news/stock-market-news/thomson-reuters-to-cut-small-number-of-engineering-jobs-4789191)

---

### What This Tells Us

The latest wave of headlines suggests AI is not only replacing tasks; it is helping firms redesign entire labor strategies, from layoffs to hiring standards to legal risk management. That combination strengthens the case for policies like UBI, because the question is no longer whether some jobs will be affected, but how many workers will face disruption before the next job is available — or whether it ever appears.
